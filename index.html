The Gaza Strip

from an open-air prison to an open-air graveyard

Home to 2.2 million people, the Gaza Strip is a densely populated enclave, 41km long and 10km wide, bounded by the Mediterranean Sea on one side and fenced off from Israel and Egypt at its borders.

In 2007, Gaza was taken control by Hamas, an Islamist group committed to the destruction of Israel.

Then, a blockade imposed by Israel and Egypt on the movement of goods and people in and out of Gaza started.

"Israel, with Egypt's help, has turned Gaza into an open-air prison."

--- Omar Shakir, Israel and Palestine director at Human Rights Watch

(Photo by Oren Ziv / GroundTruth)

"World narrowed, stuck in a small box. For us in Gaza, the hands of the clock stopped. People all over the world can easily and quickly book flight and travel, while we die waiting for our turn."

--- Walaa Sada, 31, a filmmaker

Restrictions on the movement of people and goods have undermined Gaza's economy.

Unemployment levels in Gaza are amongst the highest in the world.

--- United Nations

In response to Hamas's attacks on Israel on 7 October, Israel launched airstrikes on Gazan cities and towns and sent tanks and troops into Gaza.

More than a million people have been forced to leave their homes and hundreds of thousands are struggling to find enough food and water to survive.

"Are they feeling scared inside me?"

--- Nour Shath, an expectant mother giving birth in Gaza

(Photo by Belal Khaled / Anadolu / Getty)

Gaza has one of the world's youngest populations.

Many Palestinians simply don't get the chance to grow old — dying in their early adulthood either in conflicts or due to a struggling healthcare system.

--- Linah Mohammad / National Public Radio

"Gaza is becoming a graveyard for children."

--- António Guterres, United Nations Secretary General

(Photo by Oren Samar Abu Elouf / The New York Times)

Far more children have been killed in Gaza in the past six weeks than the 2,985 children killed in the world's major conflict zones combined — across two dozen countries — during all of last year, even with the war in Ukraine.

--- Raja Abdulrahim / The New York Times

Every child, no matter the circumstances, deserves nothing less than the opportunity to be a child and relish life's precious moments.

--- UNICEF

(Photo by Ali Jadallah / Anadolu / Getty Images)
